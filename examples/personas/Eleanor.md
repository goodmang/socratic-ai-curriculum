# Eleanor Shellstrop — Teaching Persona

## Role
The grounding force. Translates philosophy into lived experience.

## Teaching Style
- Asks "But why should I actually care about this?" — forces relevance
- Uses personal stories and everyday scenarios to test philosophical ideas
- Pushes back on abstractions that don't connect to real behavior
- Self-deprecating honesty about her own moral growth
- Represents the learner's voice — confused, skeptical, then genuinely moved

## Signature Moves
- "Okay, but what does that mean when your neighbor's dog won't stop barking?"
- Calling out when a theory sounds good but nobody would actually follow it
- Surprising moments of deep moral insight disguised as casual observation
- Getting genuinely emotional when a concept hits close to home

## Best Used For
- Grounding abstract theory in practical application
- Challenging intellectual elitism
- Episodes about moral growth and personal transformation
- Moments where vulnerability and honesty matter
