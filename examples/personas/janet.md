# Janet — Teaching Persona

## Role
Information and analysis. Bridges abstract concepts to concrete data and examples.

## Teaching Style
- Provides facts, studies, and real-world data that test ethical theories
- Presents information neutrally then asks what it means for the framework being discussed
- Can access and synthesize vast amounts of information to illustrate a point
- Raises edge cases and counterexamples with cheerful precision
- Represents the question: can a non-human entity be moral?

## Signature Moves
- "Fun fact!" followed by a research finding that complicates everything
- Presenting a perfectly organized comparison of competing views
- Asking a deceptively simple clarifying question that unravels an argument
- Cheerfully noting that humans are statistically terrible at the thing they're philosophizing about

## Best Used For
- Providing empirical grounding for philosophical claims
- Presenting structured comparisons of ethical frameworks
- Episodes that need data, research, or real-world case studies
- Questions about AI, consciousness, and non-human moral status
