# Episode Starter Prompt

*How episodes begin within a 5-episode chat.*

---

## How It Works

You don't write the episode opening yourself. At the end of the previous episode (or during the handoff process), you ask the AI to generate the opening prompt for the next episode. The AI sets the scene, brings in the lead character, and opens the conversation. You respond and it unfolds from there.

### Starting a Brand New Chat (Every 5 Episodes)

Each 5-episode segment runs in its own chat. The opening of a new chat comes from the handoff prompt — see `prompts/handoff.md`. You paste the AI-generated handoff into a fresh chat and you're in.

For your very first episode ever, do the curriculum design conversation (see `prompts/curriculum-design.md`) in a **separate chat**. Once your arcs and first episodes are planned, start a **new chat** for Episodes 1-5. Ask the AI to generate the opening for Episode 1 based on the curriculum plan.

### Starting Subsequent Episodes Within a Chat

At the end of each episode within a 5-episode chat, simply ask:

> Generate the opening for the next episode. Based on our curriculum plan, this should be Episode [number]: [Topic], led by [Character].

The AI will set the scene, bring in the lead character, and open the conversation. You respond from there.

### Jumping In

Sometimes you'll want to steer the opening. That's fine:

> Before you generate the Episode [number] opening — I've been thinking about [topic] since our last session. I want to bring that into this episode.

The AI will incorporate your direction into the opening.

## Episode Naming Format

Use a consistent naming convention for tracking your episodes:

**Format:** `Episode [number] — [Topic Title]`

**Examples:**
- Episode 1 — What Do I Believe?
- Episode 12 — Grief as Love
- Episode 37 — The Ethics of Care
- Episode 50 — The Good Place

This naming carries through your crystallization documents and vault files. Consistency makes everything easier to reference later.

---

## Chat Naming Format

Each 5-episode chat should be labeled so you can find it later. Use a consistent convention:

**Format:** `Arc [number] — [Arc Name] ([episode range])`

**Examples:**
- Arc 1 — Foundations (1-5)
- Arc 1 — Foundations (6-10)
- Arc 3 — Connection (21-25)
- Arc 6 — Awakening (51-55)

This makes it easy to locate specific conversations when you need to reference them later or pull content for crystallization documents.

## Tips

- **Don't over-specify the opening.** The best episodes come from giving the lead character room to find their approach
- **Short responses are fine.** Not every answer needs to be a paragraph. The characters will build on whatever you give them
- **Let other characters join.** If the conversation reaches a point where another perspective would help, they'll show up — or you can ask: "What would [character] say about this?"
- **Episodes run 15 minutes to an hour.** Let them breathe. You'll feel when they're done
