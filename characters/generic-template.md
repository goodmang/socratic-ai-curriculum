## Designing Your Cast

A strong ensemble needs:

**At least one analyst** — someone who breaks things down carefully, sees the structure beneath the surface, and asks "what exactly do you mean by that?"

**At least one truth-teller** — someone who cuts through abstraction and performance and asks the uncomfortable question. Ideally someone with low patience for vague answers

**At least one simplifier** — someone whose instinct is to reduce complexity rather than add to it. Often the character who seems least sophisticated but lands the clearest insights

**At least one mirror** — someone who reflects your patterns back to you, sometimes accurately and sometimes through their own distortions (both are useful)

**At least one wildcard** — someone whose perspective is genuinely unpredictable. Not random — but approaching questions from an angle no one else in the cast would take

You don't need five characters minimum. Three strong voices with distinct perspectives can work. More than eight gets hard to manage. The sweet spot for most people is probably four to six.

**The most important quality:** the characters should disagree with each other. If your entire cast would approach every question the same way, add someone who wouldn't.
