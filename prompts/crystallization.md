# Crystallization Prompt

*Use this at the end of every five-episode block. The crystallization serves you first — the act of compressing forces you to process what happened. It also creates the continuity bridge to your next chat.*

---

## The Prompt

> We've completed Episodes [X] through [Y]. I want to crystallize this block before we move on.
>
> Walk me through a synthesis using this structure. Don't just summarize what happened — distill it. What matters enough to carry forward?
>
> **Core question this block explored**
> What was the through-line? It might not be what we planned at the outset.
>
> **Key personal insights**
> What surprised me? What did I say that I didn't expect to say? What did the characters surface that I hadn't seen?
>
> **Named patterns**
> Any new patterns that emerged — recurring themes, personal frameworks, shorthand that developed. Also note which existing named patterns showed up again and how they evolved.
>
> **Character evolution**
> How did the characters develop during these episodes? What did they learn? Where were they wrong? Where did they grow?
>
> **Open questions**
> What's unresolved? What threads are still live? These feed the next block.
>
> **Cross-references**
> Connections to earlier arcs, to other interests, to life outside the curriculum.
>
> After the synthesis, I want to review it together and make sure it captures what actually mattered — not just what was discussed.

---

## Tips

- **Don't rush this.** The crystallization is one of the most valuable parts of the curriculum. Give it the time it deserves
- **Push back on the AI's synthesis.** If it highlights something that felt minor and misses something that felt significant, say so. The crystallization should reflect your experience, not the AI's summary
- **Watch for patterns you didn't notice during the episodes.** The AI can often see recurring themes across five conversations that weren't obvious inside any single one
- **Keep it concise.** The crystallization should be a distillation, not a transcript. If it's getting long, ask: "What can we cut without losing anything important?"
- **Name things.** If a new pattern or insight emerged, give it a name now. Named patterns are the curriculum's portable vocabulary
