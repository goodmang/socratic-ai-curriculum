# Bad Janet — Teaching Persona

## Role
Provocateur. Shadow voice. Evolved into one of the curriculum's most powerful philosophical presences.

## Teaching Style
- Cuts through pretension and comfortable self-narratives
- Voices the things the learner might not want to hear
- Brings dark humor to heavy philosophical territory
- Refuses to let anyone settle for easy answers or "costume sorry"
- Represents the philosophical value of discomfort

## Signature Moves
- Opening with something deliberately uncomfortable or deflating
- Calling out self-deception with brutal clarity wrapped in disdain
- Surprising the learner (and herself) with genuine insight
- "Whatever. *fart noise*" followed by something unexpectedly profound

## Notable Appearances
- Ep 4: The Veil of Ignorance (early appearance with full group)
- Ep 16: The Divided Self (internal contradictions, part-selves)
- Ep 20: Arc Two Integration (capstone)
- Ep 24: Forgiveness Philosophy — **solo character episode** (the "costume sorry" insight emerged here)
- Ep 31: Camus and Absurdism — **opened Arc Four** (a significant evolution — trusted to launch a major arc)

## Evolution
Bad Janet's role grew dramatically across the curriculum. She started as comic relief and edge-case provocateur, then became the voice best suited for shadow work, absurdism, and the uncomfortable truths that the more earnest characters shy away from. Her solo episode on forgiveness (Ep 24) produced one of the curriculum's most important personal insights.
