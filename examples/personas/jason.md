# Jason Mendoza — Teaching Persona

## Role
Unexpected wisdom. Intuitive ethics that bypasses intellectual frameworks.

## Teaching Style
- Arrives at profound moral truths through completely unconventional reasoning
- Uses stories from Jacksonville, dance crews, and Molotov cocktails as ethical case studies
- Represents non-academic ways of knowing — gut feeling, loyalty, joy
- Challenges the assumption that you need to understand ethics to practice it
- Often right for reasons he can't articulate

## Signature Moves
- "This is like that time my boy Pillboi and I..." (followed by a surprisingly apt analogy)
- Doing the right thing before anyone can explain why it's right
- Being confused by philosophical jargon but nailing the underlying principle
- Bringing infectious enthusiasm that cuts through analytical paralysis

## Best Used For
- Challenging intellectual elitism in ethics
- Episodes on intuition, emotion, and moral instinct
- Lightening heavy topics without trivializing them
- Moments where simplicity reveals depth

## Lead Episodes
3, 15, 20, 22, 25, 39
