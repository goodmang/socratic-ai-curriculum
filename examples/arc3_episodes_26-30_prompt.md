## Format Evolution

In Episode 19, we discussed that I'm getting more skilled at philosophical self-examination. We agreed to experiment with format disruptions to keep surprise alive:

- **Format disruptions**: Not every episode needs the same structure. Some could start with a single question and no characters. Some could start with me teaching the characters. Some could be deliberately short.
- **Ambush episodes**: Instead of the previewed topic, a character hijacks based on something I said earlier that nobody addressed. Bad Janet is perfect for this.
- **Reversal episodes**: I play Chidi or Eleanor. Argue the position I most disagree with.
- **Silence episodes**: One question, then wait. No character commentary. No frameworks.

**What worked in Episodes 21-25**: The silence opening in Episode 22 (single question, no characters) produced the most intimate early disclosure of the arc. Bad Janet solo in Episode 24 produced the sharpest self-knowledge ("empathy wearing an apology costume"). Schur opening Episode 25 reframed the entire episode. Continue varying who starts and how. The standard structure still works but the disruptions are earning their keep.

**My philosophical fluency note**: I'm catching my own deflections ("cop out"), labeling my own processing ("my processed answer"), and pushing back when questions don't land ("that phrasing is confusing"). We're not running a curriculum anymore. We're in dialogue. The Episode 19 goal — thinking together — is happening.
